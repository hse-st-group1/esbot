## UX-Factors

#### Transperency of AI responses:
**Definition:** AI responses are explained thoroughly and structured in a understandable way. Sources for individual responses are given if possible. AI-Model rules and training data are known.

**Reasoning:** AI responses should be examined closely, due to the possibility of AIs to make mistakes.
A good structure of the AI response helps with understanding the origin of the answer. Provided sources may help with validating given information. Together this process can help critically analyse a topic or question and getting to the right conclusions. 

**Example interaction:** A student asks for an detailed explanation of a topic. The AI provides a well structured and understandable explanation with sources. The student can understand the process behind the AI-model and validate the answer with the given sources, enhancing the effectiveness of the learning process.

#### Intuitive Operation:
**Definition:** The product is is usable without prior training.  

**Reasoning:**  Intuitive operation is vital for the ESBot application to make a good first impression. Students need to see the value of the ESBot in short amount of time spent or else its unlikely they will use it in their daily studying journey. Furthermore the ESBot has to compete with other chat bots that provide a intuitive operation.

**Example Interaction:** A new student wants to ask a question to ESBot. The interface should be easily understandable or even familiar from other applications. For example a chat interface like its provided by many messengers.

#### Simplicity:
**Definition:** Easy to navigate between functions and use them.

**Reasoning:** A simple UI helps with the intuitive operation and makes students learning experience with ESBot easier. Due to ESBots focused nature and small scale a simple design should be easy accomplished. 

**Example interaction:** A user wants to generate a quiz to gain some exercise. Questions should be asked one after the other with indication how many questions he has already answered and how many are left. The student can focus at one question at a time, and after he finished the quiz the review is generated. 


#### Accessibility:
**Definition:** Accessible to diffrent kind of users despite of their cognitive, sensory and physical capabilities. 

**Reasoning:** The ESBot application implies to be a service provides by the University of applied science Esslingen for their students. According to this it could fall under the [BITV2.0](https://www.barrierefreiheit-dienstekonsolidierung.bund.de/Webs/PB/DE/gesetze-und-richtlinien/bitv2-0/bitv2-0-node.html) regulations for accessible software design.

**Example interaction:** A visually impared person is asking ESBot a question. The resulting chat interface should be structered in a way, which can be interpreted by a screen reader, so the user can access the answers and revisit his conversation in a later session.
